Something About Me

You wouldn't be able to tell if you looked at me, but I have an artificial right hip joint. I got it when I was 20. These things are great! The bone had been necrosed because of the steroids I was taking for years for Lupus Nephritis, which led to the hypercortisolism and resultant osteoporosis. Range of motion and walking were a nightmare!

After getting over the hurdle of surgery and physical therapy, for which I admittedly usually have low compliance, I felt like a whole new person! This technology and medicine made such a difference and I am so so grateful.


(Picture taken from OrthoInfo)

Another neeto device that's really helped my are my scleral lenses. They're really big compared to normal contact lenses. For me, I use them, not for vision, but comfort. They act like prosthetic corneas because mine have uneven surfaces. The lenses make sure nothing touches my cornea and prevents irritating friction from my eyelids.
